Process

━

A glimpse inside my design process, from ideation to MVP.

  • Before even discussing design, attempt to understand what success means to the client. Find out where they hope to be five years from now. Getting the long view will help ensure the client and designer's goals aren't misaligned.

    Get a sense of the business

  • Dig deeper. Where are users before, during and after using your app or website? What are their goals and motivations? Construct user journeys with storyboards to get a better sense of a typical user's flow through your product.

    Identify user needs

  • The fun part. User test your hypothesis and prototype to answer those questions. Start with an extended pen & paper sketching session, followed by low-fi wireframes in Photoshop or Sketch. Finally, move on to hi-fidelity prototyping until you've gained confidence in certain parts of the flow.

    Prototype and test

  • Work with front-end devs to make the design a reality. You're aiming for an MVP that you can learn from once it's on the market. After you've gained enough feedback, rip out/add/modify and test it all again. Then set it free.

    Design visual systems

About Me

I devise, design and occasionally develop smart and accessibile user experiences. I own a master's degree in interactive publishing from Northwestern's Medill School, and currently work as UX Designer at Fresh Tilled Soil, a firm that's been a pioneer of the user experience discipline for more than a decade. I believe in creating products based on user's needs, behaviors and habits, and not being afraid to build (and fail) early, because the best ideas are never your first. Other mantras: steal like an artist, don't make users think, and always have a plan - without one you have no destination, which means you can never arrive.
