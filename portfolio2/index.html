Work

Barreling Ahead
My capstone project at Medill. Was tasked with producing a one-page, immersive, fully interactive story. The topic of choice was the craft beer industry. While all students were required to use Bootstrap as a base, I hand-coded the remainder of the site. Discovered the greatness of jQuery's smooth scroll, and can now hold my own in conversations with most beer snobs.

North By Northwestern
A three-month project redesigning the homepage and article page for website of Northwestern's magazine. Extensive iterating and wireframing in InDesign/Photoshop/pen & paper for all device sizes, considerable typography research and hand-coding each page from scratch.

Backstory
Backstory is an app that allows users to experience a succinct timeline of a news story's events in a single view. Created by myself and three others over 36 hours at a Society for News Design hackathon. It's cross-device compatible, and features UI thats provides readers with a lightweight, engaging way to interact with a story.

Writing Clips
M.S. in Interactive Publishing from Northwestern's Medill School of Journalism. Background consists primarily of positions at sports & lifestyle publications. Featured in USA Today, Yahoo! Sports and CBS, and currently write weekly columns for BaseballHQ.com. Clips available upon request.

BaseballHQ Landing Page
Prototype for a new landing page for BaseballHQ.com. Utilizes a variety of UX best practices to induce clicks and gain readers (near completion).

Cutler Screen Crack
An experimentation with CSS3 animation that speaks to Jay Cutler's arm strength. Hover over the football for the effect. And wherever your volume is right now, cut it in half.

About

A mentor once told that me in order to become skilled at anything, you have to try it, and you have to be bad at it. That was my barrier into design and development for years - the fear of being bad. Many sleepless nights and duct-tape websites later, I've amassed a considerable skill-set in design and code to complement a master's degree in journalism. I can be an asset in all stages of the design process - from wireframing in Photoshop and InDesign to rapidly coding a prototype in the browser, I blur the line between designer and developer and can act as a liason between both specialties. I also prefer amber beers and am a fantasy sports junkie. Below you'll see I'm on the social media, and have regularly updated Github and Dribbble accounts.You can email me here.
