Let me elaborate

I'm what some might call a "journo-coder," which basically means I can code your website and write some nifty content for it too. I can also carve up wireframes in Balsamiq or InDesign, add life to them in Photoshop and rapidly code said wireframes in the browser with minimal turnaround time.

I recently earned a master's degree in interactive publishing from this fine institution. On the side, I write weekly columns about fantasy baseball.

  • Backstory

    Backstory is an app that allows users to experience a succinct timeline of a news story's events in a single view. Created by myself and three others over 36 hours at a Society for News Design hackathon. It's cross-device compatible, and features UI thats provides readers with a lightweight, engaging way to interact with a story.

    Take me there.

  • North by Northwestern

    A three-month project redesigning the homepage and article page for website of Northwestern's magazine. I performed extensive iterating and wireframing in InDesign/Photoshop/pen & paper for all device sizes, and did considerable typography research. Both pages were hand-coded from scratch.

    Take me there.

  • Barreling Ahead

    My capstone project at Medill. Was tasked with producing a one-page, immersive, fully interactive story. The topic of choice was the craft beer industry. While all students were required to use Bootstrap as a base, I hand-coded the remainder of the site. Discovered the greatness of jQuery's smooth scroll, and can now hold my own in conversations with most beer snobs.

    Take me there.

  • BaseballHQ Landing Page

    A prototype for a new landing page for BaseballHQ.com. Utilizes a variety of UX best practices to induce clicks and gain readers (near completion).

    Take me there.

  • Jay Cutler Screen Break

    An experimentation with CSS3 animation that speaks to Jay Cutler's arm strength. Hover over the football for the effect. And wherever your volume is right now, cut it in half.

    Take Me There.
